Greenchild-Kiri: Patent Pending. Non-invasive.

Conventional Paulownia species can spread uncontrollably. Greenchild-Kiri cannot. Our hybrids are bred so that they can only be propagated through controlled laboratory breeding — no wild seeding, no invasive growth. This is the fundamental difference.

The Difference

Conventional Paulownia vs. Greenchild-Kiri

Conventional Paulownia

  • Uncontrolled wild seeding
  • Classified as invasive in some regions
  • Variable wood quality
  • No standardized genetics
  • Fluctuating growth rates

Greenchild-Kiri

  • No wild seeding possible
  • Patent pending — controlled propagation in the laboratory
  • Consistent, high wood quality
  • Standardized genetics through micropropagation
  • Optimized growth rates
Micropropagation

From the Lab to the Plantation

Every Greenchild tree begins as a tissue culture in the laboratory. Four steps — from cell material to finished seedling.

1

Tissue sampling

Cell material from the mother plant is taken under sterile conditions.

2

In-vitro culture

Propagation in nutrient medium under controlled light conditions.

3

Rooting

Seedlings develop their own root system in the greenhouse.

4

Acclimatization

Hardening under real conditions — ready for the plantation.

In the Greenhouse

Thousands of seedlings. Each one inspected.
